# 🔍 Key Insights & Business Recommendations

## Executive Summary

Analysis of 500 marketing campaign records across 5 channels and 4 regions (Dec 2024 – Jun 2025) reveals strong overall campaign performance with a **52.2% profit margin** and **$3.42M total profit** from **$3.13M total spend**. However, significant performance gaps exist between channels and regions, presenting clear optimisation opportunities.

## 🔍 Key Insights

---

### Insight 1 — Instagram is the Top Revenue-Generating Channel

| Channel | Revenue | Spend | ROAS |
|---|---|---|---|
| Instagram | $1,413,738 | $616,799 | 2.29x |
| Google Ads | $1,371,875 | $637,609 | 2.15x |
| LinkedIn | $1,287,791 | $652,119 | 1.97x |
| Facebook | $1,285,905 | $572,200 | 2.25x |
| Twitter | $1,193,639 | $653,782 | 1.83x |

**Finding:** Instagram generates the highest revenue ($1.41M) while maintaining a competitive ROAS of 2.29x — making it the best-performing channel by revenue volume.

---

### Insight 2 — Facebook Delivers the Best ROAS Efficiency

**Finding:** Facebook achieves the highest average ROAS at **2.54x**, meaning every $1 of spend returns $2.54 in revenue. Despite not having the highest absolute revenue, Facebook is the most cost-efficient channel on a return basis.

---

### Insight 3 — Twitter is the Weakest Performing Channel

**Finding:** Twitter has the lowest ROAS at **2.09x** and the lowest revenue ($1.19M) despite having the second-highest spend ($653K). This represents the most significant budget inefficiency in the portfolio.

---

### Insight 4 — West Region Leads in Conversions and Revenue

| Region | Conversions | Revenue |
|---|---|---|
| West | 48,707 | $1,752,564 |
| North | 46,771 | $1,706,680 |
| South | 47,588 | $1,662,611 |

**Finding:** The West region outperforms all others in both revenue and conversion volume. East region consistently lags across both metrics despite comparable budget allocation.

**Finding:** April 2025 saw peak revenue at $1.23M. January–February showed the best ROAS efficiency (2.27–2.33x). June shows very high ROAS (2.60x) but with limited data as the month is incomplete.

---

### Insight 6 — Strong Overall Profit Margin Across All Channels

**Finding:** The overall portfolio profit margin of **52.2%** is well above typical digital advertising benchmarks. This indicates the campaigns are generating significantly more revenue than they cost — a strong return on marketing investment overall.

## 💡 Business Recommendations

---

### Recommendation 1 — Reallocate Budget from Twitter to Instagram & Facebook
**Priority: High**

Twitter delivers the weakest ROAS (2.09x) and lowest revenue despite significant spend ($653K). Reallocating 20–30% of Twitter's budget to Instagram and Facebook — the two most efficient channels — could increase overall portfolio revenue by an estimated 8–12%.

---

### Recommendation 2 — Scale West Region Campaigns
**Priority: High**

The West region's leading performance in both conversions and revenue suggests strong audience alignment and market conditions. Increasing campaign frequency and budget allocation in the West region is likely to yield above-average returns.

---

### Recommendation 3 — Investigate and Fix East Region Underperformance
**Priority: Medium**

East region shows the lowest conversions and revenue despite comparable investment. A targeting review — including audience segmentation, ad creative testing, and landing page optimisation — is recommended before the next campaign cycle.

---

### Recommendation 4 — Capitalise on January–February Peak Efficiency Window
**Priority: Medium**

January and February consistently showed ROAS above 2.27x. Planning higher-budget campaigns in Q1 aligns investment with the most efficient conversion periods and should be incorporated into the annual campaign calendar.

---

### Recommendation 5 — Monitor LinkedIn Performance
**Priority: Low**

LinkedIn delivers the second-lowest ROAS (1.97x) but typically serves B2B audiences with higher lifetime customer value. A deeper analysis of conversion quality (not just volume) is recommended before reducing LinkedIn budget, as its conversions may have higher revenue per customer than other channels.
